The Reconstruction Trust — A Structured Alternative to Lump-Sum Reparations

Ukraine's reparations claim against Russia (est. USD 500–700B) is unlikely to resolve as an immediate lump-sum payment. A more durable mechanism would place Russia's frozen assets (~USD 280–300B principal, generating ~USD 3B/year in ERA proceeds) into a sovereign wealth fund — the Reconstruction Trust. Rather than a single disbursement, the Trust pays an availability payment each year: a known, predictable cash-flow stream that project financiers can use to service concessional debt now, without waiting for a final reparations settlement. This page models the Trust under three drawdown policies and two principal scenarios, so institutional financiers can size supportable debt against a credible annual payment.
